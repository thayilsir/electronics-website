Electronic Components

Resistors

Limit current flow, divide voltages, and adjust signal levels. Measured in ohms (Ω) with color-coded bands.

Capacitors

Store and release electrical energy for filtering, timing, and power stabilization. Measured in farads (F).

Transistors

Amplify or switch electronic signals. Types include BJT, FET, and MOSFET. Fundamental to modern electronics.

Inductors

Store energy in magnetic fields, resist changes in current. Used in filters, transformers, and power supplies.

Diodes

Allow current flow in one direction only. Types include LED, Zener, and Schottky diodes.

Integrated Circuits

Complex circuits miniaturized on a semiconductor chip. Range from simple timers to microprocessors.

Potentiometers

Variable resistors with adjustable resistance. Used for volume controls, light dimmers, and calibration.

Connectors

Physical interfaces for electrical connections. Include USB, HDMI, RJ45, and various terminal blocks.

LEDs

Light Emitting Diodes that emit light when current passes through. Energy-efficient lighting solution.

Oscillators

Generate repetitive electronic signals. Used in clocks, radios, and digital systems for timing.

Arduino Family

Open-source platform with easy-to-use hardware and software. Perfect for beginners and rapid prototyping.

  • Arduino Uno: Most popular for beginners
  • Arduino Nano: Compact size for small projects
  • Arduino Mega: More I/O pins for complex projects
  • Arduino Due: 32-bit ARM core for advanced applications

Raspberry Pi

Single-board computers that run full operating systems. Ideal for projects needing more computing power.

  • RPi 4: Most powerful with up to 8GB RAM
  • RPi Zero: Ultra-compact and low cost
  • RPi Pico: Microcontroller with RP2040 chip
  • GPIO Pins: 40-pin header for electronics projects

ESP32 Series

Low-cost, low-power system-on-chip microcontrollers with integrated Wi-Fi and dual-mode Bluetooth.

  • ESP32-WROOM: Standard module with Wi-Fi/BT
  • ESP32-CAM: With camera interface
  • ESP32-S2: USB OTG support
  • ESP32-C3: RISC-V architecture

PIC Microcontrollers

Family of microcontrollers made by Microchip Technology. Known for low power consumption.

  • PIC16: 8-bit for simple applications
  • PIC18: Enhanced 8-bit architecture
  • PIC24: 16-bit for more complex tasks
  • dsPIC: Digital Signal Controllers

STM32 Series

32-bit ARM Cortex-M microcontrollers from STMicroelectronics. High performance for demanding applications.

  • STM32F0: Entry-level Cortex-M0
  • STM32F4: High-performance Cortex-M4
  • STM32L0: Ultra-low-power series
  • STM32H7: Maximum performance Cortex-M7

Interview Questions & Answers

Analog vs Digital Signals

Analog signals are continuous and represent physical measurements, while digital signals are discrete and represent data as binary values (0s and 1s).

Ohm's Law

V = I × R, where V is voltage, I is current, and R is resistance. Current through a conductor is proportional to voltage and inversely proportional to resistance.

Capacitor Function

Stores electrical energy, filters noise from signals, blocks DC while allowing AC to pass, and used in timing circuits.

Transistor Types

BJT (Bipolar Junction Transistor) and FET (Field Effect Transistor). BJTs are current-controlled, FETs are voltage-controlled.

Diode Applications

Rectification (AC to DC conversion), voltage regulation (Zener diodes), light emission (LEDs), and photodetection (photodiodes).

Microcontroller vs Microprocessor

Microcontrollers have built-in memory and peripherals for embedded systems, while microprocessors require external components.

PCB Design Process

Schematic design, component placement, routing, design rule check, Gerber file generation, and manufacturing.

Op-Amp Applications

Amplification, filtering, signal conditioning, mathematical operations, and oscillation circuits.

Communication Protocols

I2C, SPI, UART for short-distance; Ethernet, WiFi, Bluetooth for longer distances and wireless communication.

Power Supply Types

Linear (low noise, less efficient) and switching (high efficiency, more noise). Selection depends on application requirements.
