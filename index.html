Popular Microcontrollers

Arduino Uno

The Arduino Uno is a popular microcontroller board based on the ATmega328P. It's perfect for beginners with its simple programming environment and extensive community support.

  • 14 Digital I/O Pins
  • 6 Analog Input Pins
  • 16 MHz Clock Speed
  • 32 KB Flash Memory
Learn More About Arduino

Raspberry Pi 4

The Raspberry Pi is a series of small single-board computers that can run a full operating system. It's great for more complex projects that require networking or multimedia capabilities.

  • Quad-core Cortex-A72 CPU
  • Up to 8GB RAM
  • Dual HDMI Outputs
  • Gigabit Ethernet
Learn More About Raspberry Pi

ESP32

The ESP32 is a series of low-cost, low-power system-on-a-chip microcontrollers with integrated Wi-Fi and dual-mode Bluetooth. Ideal for IoT projects.

  • Dual-core Tensilica LX6 CPU
  • Integrated WiFi and Bluetooth
  • Ultra-low power consumption
  • Rich peripheral interface

Electronics Engineering Interview Questions

What is the difference between analog and digital signals?

Analog signals are continuous signals that vary over time, representing physical measurements. Digital signals are discrete-time signals generated by digital modulation, representing data as a sequence of discrete values.

Explain Ohm's Law.

Ohm's Law states that the current through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage across the two points and inversely proportional to the resistance between them. It is represented by the formula: V = I × R, where V is voltage, I is current, and R is resistance.

What is the purpose of a capacitor in a circuit?

Capacitors serve several purposes in electronic circuits including storing electric charge, filtering noise from power supplies, blocking DC while allowing AC to pass (coupling), and timing elements in oscillator circuits.

What is the difference between RAM and ROM?

RAM (Random Access Memory) is volatile memory that temporarily stores data for quick access by the processor. ROM (Read Only Memory) is non-volatile memory that permanently stores firmware and system boot instructions.
